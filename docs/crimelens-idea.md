1. ## Basic information

   

| Project Name | CrimeLens \- online map for pickpocket cases in Europe |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Project Goal** | Build an online platform using maps, to make users aware what places in Europe are the most dangerous for pickpocketing and where they need to pay special attention and be most careful. This includes not only pickpocketing, but also car theft and other felonies |
| **Brief description** | This app helps users stay informed and safe in urban environments by providing detailed, up-to-date information on pickpocketing and other petty crime rates in cities and neighborhoods. Travelers and locals can use it to choose safer routes, plan accommodations, and make informed decisions about where to live or visit. Beyond crime awareness, the app includes a lost-and-found feature, allowing users to report and locate lost items and connect with finders directly. Future updates may expand to include additional municipal, civic, or law enforcement data, creating a comprehensive urban safety and community platform.  |
| **Competitor product analysis** | **Crowdsourced pickpocket alert apps Advantages:** real-time user reports; simple concept; useful in major tourist cities. **Disadvantages:** uneven coverage across Europe; data quality issues; usually limited to pickpocketing only; little context or guidance. **Curated hotspot / travel scam guides Advantages:** easy to understand; clear lists of risky areas; lightweight user experience. **Disadvantages:** static and slow to update; limited city coverage; weak transparency on data sources; poor personalization. **Broad travel safety platforms Advantages:** wide safety scope; structured alerts; perceived credibility. **Disadvantages:** too generic for tourist theft scenarios; heavy onboarding; not focused on street-level, actionable risk. **Official crime maps and indices Advantages:** authoritative data; standardized crime categories. **Disadvantages:** fragmented across countries; not tourist-oriented; lack POI-level or real-time usefulness. **crimelens advantages** crimelens focuses specifically on Europe and on the crimes most relevant to travelers (pickpocketing, car theft, other common felonies). It combines a clear, map-first UI with low-friction access via magic-link login. Multi-crime layers, visible recency, and transparent sourcing provide more actionable insight than static guides or broad safety scores. A modern, map-native tech stack enables fast performance, simple interaction, and a clearly defined purpose competitors typically lack.   |
| **List of technologies used** | HTMX for frontend leafletjs.com for maps Elysia \+ BUN for backend stack Postgres for database Resend \+ Nodemailer for email communication |
| **Description of the  technological stack and  justification of selected  technologies** | **Frontend – HTMX** HTMX enables a server-driven UI with minimal JavaScript, reducing frontend complexity while keeping the application fast and maintainable. It aligns well with a clear, map-focused product and allows rapid iteration without a heavy SPA framework. **Maps – Leaflet** Leaflet is a lightweight, open-source mapping library well suited for interactive maps with markers, clustering, and overlays. It integrates cleanly with HTMX-based pages and provides sufficient flexibility without unnecessary complexity. **Backend – Bun \+ Elysia** Bun provides high-performance JavaScript/TypeScript execution with fast startup times and an integrated toolchain. Elysia is a Bun-first web framework offering strong typing, low overhead, and efficient request handling, making it suitable for a scalable API and background tasks. **Database – PostgreSQL** PostgreSQL is a mature, reliable relational database with strong support for structured data and extensibility. It is well suited for handling geospatial data and complex queries required for crime hotspots and filtering. **Email – Resend \+ Nodemailer** Resend is optimized for fast, reliable transactional email delivery, ideal for magic-link authentication. Nodemailer provides a flexible abstraction layer for composing and sending emails, ensuring compatibility and future extensibility. |
